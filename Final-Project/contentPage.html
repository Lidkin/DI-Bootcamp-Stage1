Why Horror?

Fairy tales like Cinderella, Little Red Riding Hood, or Sleeping Beauty, heard in early childhood, always ended well. But a lot changed when I learned to read and began visiting the library independently. In the village where I grew up, there was no censorship of literature by age, so I could choose whatever I wanted. Turned out that the original fairy tales are not so cute at all ... And it was scary ... exciting! From then began a fascinating passion for the worlds of horror and surrealism: Lafcraft, Stoker, King, Lynch, and many others. I want to introduce you here to some of my favorite works. Enjoy!

It (also known as Stephen King's IT) is a 1990 ABC two-part psychological horror drama miniseries directed by Tommy Lee Wallace and adapted by Lawrence D. Cohen from Stephen King's 1986 novel of the same name. The story revolves around a predatory monster that can transform itself into its prey's worst fears to devour them, allowing it to exploit the phobias of its victims. It mostly takes the humanoid form of Pennywise, a darkly comedic clown.

Clive Barker came to prominence in the mid-1980s with a series of short stories, the Books of Blood, which established him as a leading horror writer. He has since written many novels and other works. His fiction has been adapted into films, notably the Hellraiser series, the first installment of which he also wrote and directed.
When Books of Blood was first published in the United States in paperback, Stephen King was quoted on the book covers: "I have seen the future of horror and his name is Clive Barker."
Cenobites are extra-dimensional beings who appear in the novella The Hellbound Heart, the sequels The Scarlet Gospels and Hellraiser: The Toll, and the eleven Hellraiser films.
